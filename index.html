Overview

This portfolio demonstrates a comprehensive approach to evaluating visual content, including both images and videos. The evaluation process is guided by clearly defined rubrics, modules, and detailed guidelines to ensure objectivity, consistency, and high-quality standards. Each item is analyzed for instruction adherence, visual quality, motion or audio consistency, and overall composition.

The portfolio is divided into several sections covering rubric definitions, detailed case studies for videos, evaluation modules, and guidelines for both image and video bursts. Each section provides step-by-step explanations of how content is reviewed, what criteria are considered, and how final selections are made. By following these structured methods, evaluators are able to provide reliable and actionable feedback for improving outputs or selecting the most suitable content for sharing or publication.

The goal of this portfolio is to highlight a disciplined evaluation process that is transparent, repeatable, and defensible. It shows how minor defects, instruction compliance, and overall presentation are considered together to select the best outputs, whether in video or image formats.

Rubric Framework

The rubric defines the primary criteria used to evaluate both images and videos. Each criterion has a defined scale and description to ensure consistent scoring across all evaluators.

Instruction: Measures how accurately the output follows the prompt or request. An output may be visually appealing but fail to meet the task requirements if it does not follow instructions. Motion: Evaluates smoothness, continuity, and realism of movement in videos or animated content. Irregular movements, frame skipping, or unnatural transitions reduce scores. Audio: Assesses alignment, clarity, and synchronization of sound with visual events. Audio defects, noise, or mismatches impact the quality of the evaluation. Composition: Focuses on framing, visual balance, and aesthetics. Proper composition ensures the subject is clear and visually pleasing, without distracting artifacts or poor alignment.

Criterion Description Scale
Instruction Matches prompt accurately and completely 1–5
Motion Movement is smooth, consistent, and natural 1–5
Audio Sync, clarity, and quality of sound 1–5
Composition Visual balance, framing, and presentation 1–5

Evaluation Modules

The evaluation process is supported by several modules that systematically review content. Each module focuses on a specific aspect of output quality.

  • Module 1: Motion Analysis – Detects inconsistencies in object movement, missing frames, or unnatural transitions.
  • Module 2: Audio Sync – Ensures audio tracks align correctly with visual events, including dialogue, sound effects, and background music.
  • Module 3: Composition Check – Reviews framing, balance, and aesthetics to ensure outputs meet visual standards.
  • Module 4: Instruction Compliance – Compares outputs against the original prompt to verify correct execution of requested edits or generation tasks.

Using these modules allows evaluators to break down complex visual and audio outputs into measurable components, which improves scoring consistency and supports objective decision-making.

Image Edit Guidelines

  • Evaluate image pairs based on an original image and a specific edit prompt.
  • Assess instruction adherence, overall preference, and any defects.
  • Provide concise justification when required.
  • Avoid ties like "About the same" whenever possible.
  • Select "Overall Best" based on quality, instruction adherence, and shareability. Avoid images with severe defects or unprompted watermarks.

Image Generation Guidelines

  • Evaluate 2–4 images generated from a text prompt.
  • Assess each independently for instruction adherence and overall preference.
  • Choose "Overall Best" as the image that is most shareable and meets quality standards.
  • Prefer photorealistic outputs unless a specific style is requested.
